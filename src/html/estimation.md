## [Standish Group 2015 Chaos Report](https://www.infoq.com/articles/standish-chaos-2015)

Smaller projects have a much higher likelihood of success than larger ones:

| Project Size | Successful | Challenged | Failed |
|--------------|------------|------------|--------|
| Grand        | 2%         | 7%         | 17%    |
| Large        | 6%         | 17%        | 24%    |
| Medium       | 9%         | 26%        | 31%    |
| Moderate     | 21%        | 32%        | 17%    |
| Small        | 62%        | 16%        | 11%    |
| **Total**    | **100%**   | **100%**   | **100%** |


Ranked success factors:

| Factors of Success            | Points | Investment |
|-------------------------------|--------|------------|
| Executive Sponsorship         | 15     | 15%        |
| Emotional Maturity            | 15     | 15%        | the collection of basic behaviors of how people work together
| User Involvement              | 15     | 15%        | users are involved in the project decision-making and information-gathering process
| Optimization                  | 15     | 15%        | managing scope based on relative business value
| Skilled Resources             | 10     | 10%        |
| Standard Architecture         | 8      | 8%         |
| Agile Process                 | 7      | 7%         |
| Modest Execution              | 6      | 6%         |
| Project Management Expertise  | 5      | 5%         |
| Clear Business Objectives     | 4      | 4%         |
